Let’s Think During Reasoning: Adapting Stage-wise Self-consistent Plan-and-Solve for ScienceQA

Paper Code Datasets

Abstract

Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting enhances reasoning but suffers from its single-path nature, often missing steps or causing semantic drift. Existing methods also struggle to effectively fuse multimodal information, limiting their capability on complex tasks like ScienceQA. To address this, we propose Think-ScienceQA, a stage-wise plan-and-solve framework with a semantic adapter. It generates multiple reasoning candidates and applies stage-wise self-consistency to select coherent answers. The lightweight semantic adapter bridges frozen visual encoders and language models for improved multimodal alignment. Despite using only 228M parameters (≈6% of the best fine-tuned model), Think-ScienceQA achieves state-of-the-art results on ScienceQA and A-OKVQA. Ablation studies show the planning and self-consistency modules contribute 6.7% and 4.1% performance gains, respectively.

Performance vs. Model Size

The scatter plot illustrates the strong performance–efficiency trade-off of Think-ScienceQA. The large variant achieves 95.59% accuracy, while the base model reaches 93.86%, both surpassing LLaVA (90.92%), LLaVA+GPT-4 (92.53%), and LLaMA-Adapter (85.19%). Notably, Think-ScienceQAbase outperforms the larger Multimodal-CoTlarge (91.68%), and Think-ScienceQAlarge achieves even better results with a comparable model size, emphasizing the framework’s scalability and reasoning strength without model over-expansion.
