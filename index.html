Let’s Think During Reasoning: Adapting Stage-wise Self-consistent Plan-and-Solve for ScienceQA

Paper Code Datasets

Abstract

Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting improves reasoning performance but is inherently constrained by its single-path paradigm, often leading to missing steps and semantic misunderstandings. Moreover, existing approaches struggle to effectively integrate multi-modal information and maintain coherent reasoning, limiting their performance on complex multi-modal tasks in ScienceQA. To overcome these challenges, this paper introduces Think-ScienceQA, a stage-wise plan-and-solve self-consistent reasoning framework guided by a semantic adapter, which explicitly thinks stage by stage to perform planned reasoning in ScienceQA tasks. Think-ScienceQA generates multiple candidate reasoning paths and employs a stage-wise self-consistency mechanism to identify the most coherent and reliable solution. In addition, a lightweight semantic adapter is designed to bridge the semantic gap between frozen visual encoders and language models, enabling more precise and efficient multi-modal feature alignment. Despite having only 228M parameters, approximately 6% the size of the strongest fine-tuned baseline, Think-ScienceQA achieves state-of-the-art performance on both the ScienceQA and A-OKVQA benchmarks. Extensive ablation studies demonstrate that the stage-wise reasoning design and self-consistency mechanism contribute performance gains of 6.7 and 4.1 percentage points, respectively.

Pipeline Overview

Dataset Re-annotation

To build a high-quality training set for Think-ScienceQA, this paper re-annotates ScienceQA & A-OKVQA with GPT-4o to create a high-quality training set. Each sample now contains four structured components—Summary, Caption, Reasoning, Conclusion. The overview details the generation, validation, and filtering procedures for these rationales.

Step 1: Re-annotation Overview

Use GPT-4o to produce four-stage rationales: Summary → Caption → Reasoning → Conclusion.

Step 2: Prompt Design

Carefully-crafted prompts ensure consistent and structured output.

Step 3: Format Validation

Filter out responses that deviate from the required four-stage structure.

Performance vs. Model Size

After extensive experimentation across various model scales and training setups, we present a representative visualization of the Performance–Size Trade-off to highlight the scalability and efficiency of Think-ScienceQA.

The scatter plot shows that Think-ScienceQA achieves an excellent performance–size balance. Think-ScienceQAlarge attains 95.59% accuracy, and Think-ScienceQAbase reaches 93.86%—both outperforming LLaVA (90.92%), LLaVA+GPT-4 (92.53%), and LLaMA-Adapter (85.19%).

Moreover, Think-ScienceQAbase surpasses the larger Multimodal-CoTlarge (91.68%), demonstrating strong reasoning even at smaller scales. Think-ScienceQAlarge further outperforms it with similar parameter counts, highlighting our approach’s efficiency and scalability without requiring massive model growth.
